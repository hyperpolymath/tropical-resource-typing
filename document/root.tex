\title{Tropical Algebra: Max-Plus and Min-Plus Semirings}
\author{Jonathan D.A.\ Jewell}
\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
We formalise the tropical semiring in two flavours: the max-plus semiring
$(\mathbb{N} \cup \{-\infty\},\, \max,\, +)$ and the min-plus semiring
$(\mathbb{N} \cup \{+\infty\},\, \min,\, +)$, both instantiated in
Isabelle's \texttt{comm\_semiring\_1} hierarchy as dioids.

Building on this foundation we develop matrix algebra over both semirings,
characterise matrix powers as tropical sums over walk sets, and prove
a Floyd-Warshall theorem: under the absence of positive-weight cycles the
Kleene star of a matrix equals the maximum-weight simple-path matrix.

The Kleene star is defined finitely as $(I \oplus A)^{n-1}$ (no infinitary
construction required) and shown to satisfy the standard star equation
$A^* = I \oplus A \cdot A^*$ and to be the least prefixpoint of
$X \mapsto I \oplus A \cdot X$.

Finally, we instantiate the \emph{Certified Null Operation} (CNO)
predicate from the absolute-zero resource model: we exhibit six CNO
instances for tropical matrices (\texttt{id}, $I \oplus {-}$,
$A \oplus A$, $A^*$, and their compositions), prove that CNOs satisfy
$\Delta S = 0$, and derive that the second application of $A^*$ costs
$O(0)$ computation.
\end{abstract}
