== What it is (as we use it)

In this repository, "tropical" names a *resource-grade axis*: a single,
machine-checked interface — `Resource.ResourceAlgebra` (an ordered resource
semiring, layered as `Resource.ResourceSemiring` + a monotone preorder) — together
with concrete carriers that instantiate it. The interface fixes exactly the
algebra a generic resource-typing soundness proof depends on:

* a carrier `R` with `zero`, `one`, choice `⊞` (`add`) and sequential composition
  `⊠` (`mul`), satisfying the semiring laws;
* a grade order `⊑` (`le`) that is a *preorder* (reflexive, transitive — the
  weakest order that supports subsumption / subtyping of grades), with both
  operations *monotone* with respect to it.

Antisymmetry is deliberately *not* in the core; it is an optional refinement
(`PartialOrderedResourceAlgebra`). The interface requires no finite carrier, no
additive idempotence, no multiplicative commutativity, no decidable equality.

The *tropical* instances are particular carriers of this one interface:
`Resource.Instances.MaxPlus` (`ℕ ∪ {−∞}`, `⊞ = max`, `⊠ = +`),
`Resource.Instances.MinPlus` (`ℕ ∪ {+∞}`, `⊞ = min`, `⊠ = +`), and
`Resource.Instances.MinMax` — the *bottleneck* algebra (`⊞ = min`, `⊠ = max`) —
sitting beside the finite `{0,1,ω}` usage carriers `Linear` and `Affine`. For the
idempotent (tropical / dioid) carriers the order is the *canonical* dioid order
`a ⊑ b ⇔ a ⊞ b = b` (built once in `Resource.Algebra.Dioid`, not re-derived per
instance).

Two facts make this more than a definition. First, transport is proven
*parametric over the interface*: `Resource.parametric_resource_transport`
discharges the consumer obligation bundle `ConsumerLawBundle R` for *any*
`[ResourceAlgebra R]`, never inspecting a carrier — so a downstream proof that
quotes only `ConsumerLawBundle` holds at linear, affine, max-plus, min-plus and
min-max *at once*, with no algebraic fact re-proved. Per the in-session axiom
audit recorded in `AFFIRMATION.adoc`, that theorem depends on *no axioms at all*.
Second, *cost composition is tropical matrix algebra*: `Resource.Closure` builds
the `n × n` matrix layer over an arbitrary `[ResourceAlgebra R]` — matrix product,
powers, and the *Kleene star / closure* — so that at max-plus the closure is
classical all-pairs least-cost (Floyd–Warshall), and at min-max it is the
all-pairs bottleneck closure whose corollary is `hub_ceiling`: along a composed
adapter path the worst link *floors* the achievable fidelity.

That is what "tropical" is here: not a free-standing algebra of interest for its
own sake, but the *grade* of a resource type system, used carrier-polymorphically
to bound worst-case cost and protocol round-counts.

== The established structure it instantiates (and where it is from)

The mathematics is entirely standard. The objects above are, in order:

*Idempotent semirings / dioids.* `ResourceAlgebra` is an ordered semiring; the
tropical carriers are *dioids* (semirings with idempotent addition), whose
canonical order `a ⊑ b ⇔ a ⊞ b = b` is the textbook dioid order. The canonical
references are Gondran and Minoux, _Graphs, Dioids and Semirings: New Models and
Algorithms_ (Springer, 2008), and Golan, _Semirings and their Applications_
(Kluwer, 1999).

*The (max,+) / tropical algebra.* The max-plus and min-plus carriers are the
classical (max,+) and (min,+) semirings. The canonical systematic treatment is
Baccelli, Cohen, Olsder and Quadrat, _Synchronization and Linearity: An Algebra
for Discrete Event Systems_ (Wiley, 1992); see also the survey work of Gaubert and
collaborators on max-plus algebra and its applications.

*Kleene algebra and the star / closure.* The matrix closure layer is the Kleene
star of a (matrix) semiring; all-pairs least-cost as a star is the standard
Floyd–Warshall-as-closure result. The canonical references are Conway, _Regular
Algebra and Finite Machines_ (Chapman & Hall, 1971), and Kozen, "A Completeness
Theorem for Kleene Algebras and the Algebra of Regular Events" (_Information and
Computation_, 1994).

*Tropical geometry* (for the broader name and intuition). The "tropical" naming
and the min/max-plus semiring viewpoint are surveyed in Maclagan and Sturmfels,
_Introduction to Tropical Geometry_ (American Mathematical Society, GSM 161, 2015).

*Quantitative and graded type theory* (the typing side). Grading a type system by
a semiring is established: Atkey, "The Syntax and Semantics of Quantitative Type
Theory" (LICS 2018), building on McBride, "I Got Plenty o' Nuttin'" (in _A List of
Successes That Can Change the World_, Springer LNCS 9600, 2016); and the coeffect /
graded-modal line of Petricek, Orchard and Mycroft (coeffects), and Gaboardi,
Katsumata, Orchard, Breuvart and Uustalu, "Combining Effects and Coeffects via
Grading" (ICFP 2016). Our `⊠`/`⊞`/`⊑` interface is precisely a usage/resource
semiring of grades in this sense.

These citations are made conservatively: where we are not certain of an exact
title or year we have named the work at the level of the author and venue rather
than invent precision. None of the structures above is ours.

== What is ours, and what is not (the contribution boundary)

What is *borrowed* is everything in the previous section: dioids and the canonical
dioid order, the (max,+) and (min,+) semirings, Kleene/closure and Floyd–Warshall,
the tropical viewpoint, and semiring-graded typing.

What is *ours* is the *modelling*: applying the dioid as the **grade of a resource
type system**, exposed as one *carrier-polymorphic* interface, with transport
proved *once* over that interface and reused at every carrier, aimed at worst-case
cost analysis and protocol round-bounds. Concretely — and only as far as the
ground-truth `AFFIRMATION.adoc` checked this session affirms:

* `Resource.parametric_resource_transport` discharges `ConsumerLawBundle R` for an
  arbitrary `[ResourceAlgebra R]` and, on the in-session `#print axioms` audit,
  *depends on no axioms at all* — so the same downstream soundness proof
  instantiates at linear, affine, max-plus, min-plus and min-max without
  re-proving any algebraic fact (`Resource.Stress`, `Resource.Conformance`).
* The Lean library `lake build`s green from source with no Mathlib (`import Init`
  only), 20/20 steps, with *no* `sorry` / `admit` / bare `axiom` /
  `native_decide`; the headline theorems `soundness`, the QTT refinement
  `tropical_grade_le_sequentialTotal`, and the bottleneck corollary `hub_ceiling`
  audit to `propext` (+ `Quot.sound`) only.
* `Resource.Closure` lifts the closure math generically to the matrix level over
  *any* `ResourceAlgebra`, so all-pairs least-cost (max-plus) and all-pairs
  bottleneck (min-max, `hub_ceiling`) are two instantiations of one proof.
* The axis is *consumed* downstream: it is the resource-grade axis carried into
  `typed-wasm` (L11).

That is the whole of the claimed usefulness: a kernel-clean, carrier-polymorphic
grading library with a checkable transport theorem and a checkable consumer. It is
*not* a claim to a verified production type checker — `AFFIRMATION.adoc` is
explicit that the proved calculus is an idealised core with no executable checker
wired to it.

**On contribution (the same stance in every sibling repo).** We did not invent the
mathematics this work rests on. Graded monads, tropical / idempotent dioids,
multiparty session types and choreographies, and epistemic / justification logic
are established structures authored by others, cited above. What is ours is the
*modelling*: we apply these structures as instruments fitted to specific domains —
verifiable WebAssembly, federated identity, BEAM sandboxing, structured
information loss — in places the established corpus had not been instantiated this
way, and we report only where the fit has already produced *checkable artefacts*.
The contribution is the model and its demonstrated usefulness, not the underlying
mathematics. This is deliberate, and it is a strength: a claim that concedes its
own foundations is survivable; one that claims invention dies at the first
citation. (Cf. the estate discipline — echo-types' own distinctness claim was
permitted to terminate negatively, EI-2.)

== Is-not, and what is still open

To keep the boundaries un-eroded (restating the IS-NOT relations from
`FOUNDATION_CONTRACT.md`, now with the contribution frame above):

* *Tropical is a resource-algebra foundation; it is NOT the Echo modality.* It is
  not an Echo instance, and Echo is not a tropical / resource instance. The only
  sanctioned direction is `resource algebra ──consumed by──▶ residue measure`
  (`E → R` in `Resource.EchoBridge`, where the residue carrier `E` has *no*
  `ResourceAlgebra` instance); the reverse — `Echo is an instance of resource /
  soundness` — is forbidden. This repository imports no `echo-types` dependency.
* *The tropical instances are NOT the interface.* They are carriers of
  `ResourceAlgebra`; the parametric theory never case-splits a carrier. The
  infinite tropical carriers exist precisely as stress tests (`Resource.Stress`
  proves `Resource.Infinite` for them) that the abstraction is not a finite
  `{0,1,ω}` reification.
* *The Lean `Resource.*` library is authoritative.* It is the machine-checked
  source of truth (aggregator `Resource.lean`). Where any prose, the PDF note, or
  `EXPLAINME.adoc` disagrees, the Lean kernel and `AFFIRMATION.adoc` win.
* *This is NOT new mathematics.* The dioid, (max,+), Kleene closure and graded
  typing are all cited above; the contribution is the assembly and its consumer,
  not the algebra.

Honestly open (do *not* read these as green):

* *The Isabelle/HOL development is SEPARATE and KNOWN-RED.* The `ROOT` +
  `Tropical*.thy` matrix-algebra session is a different, heavyweight treatment, is
  *not* part of the Lean green claim, and per `docs/BUILD-STATUS-2026-04-26.adoc`
  has 16 proof-failure sites across 4 theories. It was *not* runnable in the
  session that wrote `AFFIRMATION.adoc` (no Isabelle on that toolchain). Exclude it
  from every green claim.
* *No production-implementation refinement proof exists.* The proved core is
  idealised; `impl/` is a single unbuilt Julia file with no build wiring, and the
  README's `cd impl/ && just build && just test` quickstart is refuted at the
  affirmed commit. The bridge from "proved calculus" to "checker in use" is
  largely absent and is not claimed.
* *The published PDF note is exposition,* not a machine-checked artefact.
